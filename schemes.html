AYUSH Startup Schemes

Scheme 1: National AYUSH Mission (NAM) +

The National AYUSH Mission aims to promote AYUSH systems through cost-effective services and integration with mainstream healthcare. Financial support is available for establishing AYUSH hospitals and dispensaries, offering opportunities for healthcare startups.

Scheme 2: AYUSH Entrepreneur Development Program (AEDP) +

The AYUSH Entrepreneur Development Program provides financial assistance and mentorship to entrepreneurs. It supports innovation, market expansion, and the development of AYUSH-based healthcare services through workshops, grants, and incubator support.

Scheme 3: AYUSH Export Promotion Council +

This scheme helps AYUSH startups expand their products internationally by providing financial aid and guidance. It supports businesses in reaching global markets, complying with international standards, and participating in trade fairs.

Scheme 4: AYUSH Oushadhi Gunvatta Evam Uttpadan Samvardhan Yojana (AOGUSY) +

AOGUSY focuses on improving the quality of AYUSH medicines. Financial support is provided to upgrade manufacturing units to meet global standards. Startups in the AYUSH pharmaceutical sector can apply for this scheme to improve product quality.

Scheme 5: AYUSH Research & Development Scheme +

This scheme encourages research in AYUSH medicine, offering financial support for startups developing evidence-based products. It also funds clinical research in Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani, and Homoeopathy, fostering innovation in healthcare.

Scheme 6: AYUSH Health and Wellness Centers (HWCs) +

AYUSH Health and Wellness Centers provide preventive and curative healthcare. Startups in the healthcare sector can manage and supply these centers, which offer AYUSH-based treatments and preventive care services to communities across India.

The Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC) offers funding for biotech startups working on healthcare innovations involving AYUSH systems. This scheme supports product development and commercialization for startups at the intersection of biotech and AYUSH.

Scheme 8: AYUSH Digital Health Program +

This scheme promotes digital health startups that develop telemedicine, mobile apps, and AI-based solutions for AYUSH healthcare. Funding and technical assistance are provided for digital innovations aimed at enhancing access to AYUSH services.

Scheme 9: AYUSH Soft Loan Scheme +

The AYUSH Soft Loan Scheme provides low-interest loans to startups for infrastructure, equipment, and working capital. It's beneficial for startups in manufacturing, service delivery, or exporting AYUSH products.

Scheme 10: Start-up India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS) +

The SISFS provides seed funding to early-stage startups, including those in AYUSH. Startups can use the fund for prototype development, product trials, and market entry, especially in health or wellness categories.

Scheme 11: SIDBI Make in India Soft Loan Fund for MSMEs (SMILE) +

SMILE provides soft loans to MSMEs, including AYUSH-based startups, focusing on manufacturing and healthcare products. It helps startups scale production and meet domestic and international demand for AYUSH products.
